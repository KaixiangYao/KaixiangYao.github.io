About

SYSTEM OVERVIEW · DIGITAL TWIN
Published Journal of Intelligent Manufacturing · 2026

Quasi-real-time digital twin for robotic assembly

Kaixiang Yao, Hao Chi, Dingye Chen, Jiwen Zhang

A physics-based multi-fidelity framework that brings full-field stress estimation into a cycle-to-cycle supervisory loop for large flexible assemblies. The system combines a non-blocking ROS 2 architecture with mesh-scale optimization to balance fidelity and latency.

5.86× speedup ≈10.7 s end-to-end ≤3.3% error vs. reference

My role ROS 2 data architecture, linearized contact constraints, mesh-scale analysis, and safety-state integration.

RUNTIME DATAFLOW · CARTILAGE
Under review CoRL · 2026

CARTILAGE: A convergence-aware rigid–soft contact runtime

Kaixiang Yao, Chao Huang, Yanlin Liu, Yao Shen

A batched runtime for contact-rich robot learning, connecting fixed-shape simulation state, GPU contact queries, convergence-aware Newton solves, continuous collision detection, and RL-facing diagnostics.

My role Soft-gripper simulation and learning framework, actuation and contact semantics, RL task design, and contact-fidelity evaluation.

PERCEPTION–ACTION LOOP · ASSISTIVE GRASPING
Under revision IEEE T-MRB · 2026

Gaze-driven perception–action control for grasp restoration

Kaixiang Yao

A gaze-triggered assistive pipeline that combines object recognition, hand-pose estimation, gesture selection, and multi-channel functional electrical stimulation for efficient grasp restoration.

My role Gaze-intention recognition, temporal filtering, perception integration, and the design of an 8-channel, 54-electrode stimulation sleeve.

CURRENT WORK

History-conditioned adaptation for dynamic rope manipulation

At UC Berkeley, I am studying how a robot can use outcomes from previous trials to adapt dynamic manipulation actions across ropes with unseen physical properties. The project combines MJLab/MuJoCo simulation, compact motion primitives, and history-conditioned representations for repeated-trial learning.

Advisor: Prof. Masayoshi Tomizuka · 2026—Present
